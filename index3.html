The Artist Behind the Legend

Exploring the life and passion of Mexico's most celebrated painter

Early Life & Tragedy

Born on July 6, 1907, in CoyoacÃ¡n, Mexico, Frida Kahlo's life was marked by physical suffering and emotional turmoil that would fuel her artistic expression. A childhood bout with polio and a devastating bus accident at 18 left her with lifelong health issues.

Artistic Journey

Confined to bed during her recovery, Frida began painting with a specially designed easel. Her self-portraits became a way to explore identity, pain, and the human condition. She painted 143 works, 55 of which were self-portraits.

Cultural Impact

Frida's work transcends art, becoming a symbol of Mexican culture, feminism, and resilience. Her unflinching self-examination and celebration of indigenous Mexican culture continue to inspire artists worldwide.
